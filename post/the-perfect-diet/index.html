The Perfect Diet

December 03, 2025
Food, Tech

This was a rough one! I asked AI to make me a perfect diet and meal plan. Getting the results was brutal, and the diet may surprise you.

Intro

In the country where I live it’s kind of hard to get cheap vitamins and even if you do somehow find them, there’s no guarantee that they won’t be fake #3rdWorldLiving.

So what’s a boy to do? Well, get all his vitamins from food, you big dumb-dumb! That way, instead of ingesting fake vitamins, I’m ingesting loads of pesticides from Chinese crops. Upgrade!

To get a perfect diet and meal plan any sane person would consult a nutritionist or at least use an app, government database or something like that as reference. Fully knowing this, I opted to use AI instead! And I’m glad I did because AI certainly affected the time it took and the difficultly of this task. Specifically, it took twice as long and was twice as hard. Nice!

Process

Let’s get started. I had a complicated process consisting of two whole steps:

  1. Make the perfect diet
  2. Make a meal plan

1) The Perfect Diet

In my AI prompt, I defined my perfect diet thusly:

* Personal considerations
    * I am 41 years old
    * I am male
    * I weigh 75kg
    * I need a low oxalate diet
    * I take no supplements, so I must get all nutrients from food
* Food assumptions
    * Assume all outputted foods are unprocessed
    * Assume all milk is whole
    * Assume all meat is raw, has skin and fat but no bones
    * Assume all fruit and vegetables are whole, completely raw and unprocessed
    * Assume potatoes are peeled
    * Assume all foods are medium-sized, except for eggs which are large
* Nutritional requirements
    * Must meet all vitamin and mineral requirements, including omega-3
    * Calorie, fat, and carb requirements may be disgarded
    * Calcium must be between 4900mg and 5600mg per week
    ein must be between 630g and 770g per week
* Sardine rules
    * I like sardines
    * They must be canned in tomato sauce
    * Must include bones
    * Must limit them to 3 cans (465g) per week
    * If sardines are included, 1 can (155g) must be consumed with 1 tablespoon of mayo and 1 slice white bread
* Food preferences
    * Low oxalate foods only!
    * Pork is good, prefer tough cuts like shoulder and leg
    * Prefer whole milk over yogurt
    * Consider foods that promote gut health
* Banned foods
    * No spinach or any leafy greens except kale or cabbage
    * No nuts, seeds, or legumes
    * No soy
    * No melon of any kind
    * No beef
    * No cottage cheese
    * No cauliflower
    * No cereals like oats or wheat
* Food limits
    * Note that just because a limit is imposed on a food, it doesn't mean that the diet is limited to that food; in fact, you can suggest better foods if needed
    * Limit potatoes to 2 per week, and they must be peeled
    * Limit eggs to 14-16 per week
    * Limit chicken to boneless thighs only, max 500g per week
    * Limit olive oil to 3 tablespoons per week
    * Limit milk to 2L per week
    * Limit hard cheese to 200g per week
    * Limit salmon to 300g per week
    * Limit raw kale to 1kg per week
    * Limit bananas to 8 per week
    * Limit avocados to 7 per week

If some of these constrictions seem weird, it’s because I’m trying to avoid inflammatory foods, oxalates, limit my calcium, get enough protein, and work around what I can get at my local market. That, and limit certain foods to reasonable amounts so that Gemini doesn’t recommend that I eat 72 bananas per day or something.

2) The Meal Plan

This is where things REALLY got difficult and I had to give up. No matter how many videos on AI prompting I watched and no matter how much feedback from Gemini I listened to, I just could not get a good result – either Gemini left out foods, made weird meals (eg, eat 1.3kg of pork on Sunday and eat 16 eggs on Monday???), or just did its own thing.

I feel like I was being clear and reasonable, but Gemini didn’t get it for some reason. You be the judge:

### NON-NEGOTIABLE FIXED SCHEDULE AND SERVING CONSTRAINTS
* Carrots (700g) & Cabbage (1200g): are mixed to create 1900g of carrots/cabbage slaw, divided into five 380g servings.
* Carrots/Cabbage Slaw: 1 serving is eaten on Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, and Saturday for a total of 5 weekly servings.
* Carrots/Cabbage Slaw should be referred to as "Slaw."
* Mushrooms: 1 serving can be eaten on Sunday, Monday, Tuesday, or Wednesday, but remember there are only THREE TOTAL SERVINGS.
* Bell peppers: 1 serving can be eaten on Sunday, Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday, but remember there are only THREE TOTAL SERVINGS.
* Avocados: 2-3 servings can be eaten on Friday or Saturday, but remember there are only FIVE TOTAL SERVINGS.
* White bread: 1 serving must be eaten on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday.
* Mayonnaise: 1 serving must be eaten on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday.
* Sardines: 1 serving must be eaten on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday.
* White bread, mayonnaise, and sardines are eaten together FOR BREAKFAST ONLY and together they are called a "Fish Sandwich."
* Nori Seaweed: 2 servings can be eaten on any day, but remember there are only SIX TOTAL SERVINGS.
* Oranges: 2-3 servings must be eaten on Monday, Wednesday, or Friday, but remember there are only SEVEN TOTAL SERVINGS.
* Potatoes: the 1 total serving must be eaten on either Monday, Wednesday, or Friday -- you choose.
* Remaining food: Disperse equally through the remaining days, keeping daily minimum protein content to 90g.

It may seem like a lot, but some things were necessary – that slaw, for example, isn’t gonna be ready for a few days, and those avocados won’t be ripe until Friday. And I don’t want to eat old-ass mushrooms or peppers, can you blame me?

So it seems like we have a situation where if you want it done right, you have to do it…without AI.
