## Background
Here are some movie/show reviews, as I've been watching a lot of these lately -- definitely not because I have free time -- but because my baby insists on napping on me, so while this happens, I check out the latest content.

## Spider Noir

I guess this is a retelling of Spiderman set in the 30s or something?  Wouldn't know because I tuned out after the first 5 minutes on account of how bad the special effects were.  IT LOOKS LIKE PS2 GRAPHICS, MAN.

Too bad because I was really looking forward to the new Nick Cage joint, and it did get a great Rotten Tomatoes score.  I guess other people aren't as picky as me when it comes to special effects?

## The Punisher: One Last Kill

Decent!  A nice little mini-story serving as an appetizer to something more substantial that will hopefully come in the future.  Punisher S01 was awesome (S02, on the other hand, just ok), but if this latest installment is characteristic of what yet's to come, I'm looking forward to it.  Nice action sequences, by the way, and definitely a step up from those in Season 2.

## Crime 101

Meh.  Just meh.

The DOP deserves plaudits for making a very visually striking movie, with nice use of colored lighting and very coherent action sequences.  Also, the editing was good, so the film never felt like it dragged.  The acting, you ask?  Also generally acceptable.  

I suppose the problem was that it was just so forgettable -- not amazing, not horrible.  Just a cute little popcorn action movie.  If you're into crime movies, and you have some time kill, and are really bored, then I suppose it won't hurt to watch it, though.

## Project Hail Mary

A technically perfect movie.  This is how sci fi should be done; it's a serious movie that doesn't take itself too seriously.  It has a long run-time but was totally engaging and well-paced so I was entertained throughout the whole thing.  1000% would see again.

## Over Your Dead Body

Couldn't tell you because I checked out after 5 minutes or so.  It's shot in that weird Hallmark style where everything looks too good.  Right up front, I also wasn't impressed by the acting or the script, so I smashed `CLOSE` on my media player pretty fast.  Oh well.

## Marty Supreme

Entertaining.  Timothee Chalamet plays a scrawny, obnoxious character, but you saw that one coming.  Joking aside, he's pretty good, as usual.  All the elements of the movie are pretty solid, and the story of the main character is bonkers.

Check it out if you have time.

## Mortal Kombat II

Well, I lasted about 30 minutes, so whoop whoop?  It just wasn't good, which is a shame because I get excited for any movie with Karl Urban, hoping it will be the next *Dredd 3D*.

Unfortunately, there was a lot wrong with MK II (the script being the worst offender), although the fight scenes were kind of fun, and some of the acting was good -- Karl Urban was good, as were a number of other actors (except whoever played Katana).  Kano was hilarious.

In summary, it's a movie that you take your kids to, although the screenwriters do try to throw in a few jokes here and there to keep the adults entertained.
