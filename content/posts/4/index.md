## Love and Hate

I have a love/hate relationship with Mexican food.  Love, because it's so delicious, but I also hate it because I wind up eating so much that I have trouble breathing, can't sleep, and stay up all night, needing to make brutal poops about 4-8 times before my body is able to purge itself of the Mexican food demons.

I'm tempted to look down at my dirty work during one of my marathon poop sessions, half expecting to see a big log wearing a sombrero and sunglasses, stroking a thick black mustache with its slender stick-like arms.  "Better lay off the jalapenos next time, eh hombre?" it cackles in a thick Mexican accent.

With horror and disgust, I reflexively slap the flush button, watching it swirl around the toilet, while the turd seems completely unfazed, wearing the same crooked grin the whole way down until it's inevitably sucked down the pipe and into the abyss.

"Remember the Alamo, bitch," I whisper through gritted teeth.

Anyway, let's talk about the individual ingredients of Mexican food.

## Minced Beef

Ok, now we're getting into more "mixed feelings" territory.  First of all, let me just say that minced beef can be great, however, eating it is also a little risky.  Let me expound.

Firstly, fuck online chefs who don't give out their beef seasoning mix.  I don't care that's is some secret recipe that's been in your family for 100 years blah blah blah.  If you're gonna start a taco recipe, you'd better finish it -- and that includes listing every ingredient in that spice mix.

Being this opaque when providing recipes is dumb.  It's like drawing someone a treasure map where the dotted line cuts out just before reaching the treasure and in its place is a little note that reads: "It's somewhere in this country, I think.  Whatever, you'll figure it out, bro."

And, no, I do not want to settle for some totally basic spice mix that you pulled out of your ass at the last minute.  That's a placeholder spice mix, more of a consolation prize than something that has the actual capacity to produce some yum yum Mexican food.  So take your 3 teaspoons of cumin, 1 teaspoon of paprika, 1 teaspoon of salt, 1 teaspoon of pepper, and 1 teaspoon of filthy lies and get them out of my face.

Yes, I'm trashing free recipes.  Yes, I know that I "get what I pay for," and I don't care.

My other problem with minced beef is that it is *wayyy* too easy to fake.

Is it soybeans?  I'm looking at YOU, Taco Bell.  

Is it wood chips? I'm still looking at you, Taco Bell.

Is it horse?  Yea, I'm still looking at Taco Bell.  What?  I wouldn't put that shit past them.

## Mole

Incredible.  It's like, the chef takes a million ingredients and mixes them together to make a sauce that tastes like something completely new, with rich and complex flavors.

My only complaint is the secrecy involved with these sauces.  Like, why do I feel like everyone who ever learned how to make a good mole had to learn some secret method over the course of a year spent in a secret submarine at the bottom of the ocean?

There's too much mystery involved here for me.  I like to taste something and know exactly what it is.  I don't want to feel like I'm trying to crack the recipe for Coca Cola every time I take a bite of something.
